Popular Phones in 2022

Most Popular Mobile Phones

Which are the most popular phone brands? In September 2021, Samsung was the world’s best-selling phone brand. With 27.55% of the total, Samsung is the world’s number one selling phone brand. In second place, again in September 2021, is Apple, with 26.75% of total sales. In third place, 15 points behind, is Xiaomi with 11.51%. In the top 15 worldwide, Nokia closes the ranking with 0.58%. Nokia, which until a few years ago was the leading brand in terms of the number of phones sold.

Most selling phone in history

Nokia was for years the brand that sold the most phones in the world. In recent years it has been overtaken by Apple and Samsung. But which are the best-selling phones in history? Among the top 10 best-selling phones in the world, eight are from the world of Nokia. At the top of the list are two ‘historic’ phones. The Nokia 1100 and the Nokia 1110. The Nokia 1100 was announced in August 2003 and by the end of the year 250 million units had already been sold. The strength of the Nokia 1100 was also given by its low cost, which allowed the mobile phone to be sold also in developing countries. Among the best-selling phones, as mentioned, was also the Nokia 1100 and the Nokia 1110i. This model, first sold in 2005, also leads the list of the world’s best-selling phones with over 250 million models sold.

This Year

Due to a confluence of factors — from production delays thanks to the ongoing pandemic, rising costs of labor and components, to the global chip shortage — prices for flagship phones are higher than ever. The absolutely premium phones, like the Apple iPhone 13 Pro Max and Samsung Galaxy Z Fold 3 5G, now cost well over $1,000, or about the price of a good laptop. Thankfully, you no longer have to spend a huge amount to get something amazing, as a bunch of great smartphones are available for around $600 to 800 these days. Our pick for the best overall phone is the $829 Apple iPhone 13, and two of Google’s Pixel phones included here lean toward midrange pricing rather than all-out flagship.
